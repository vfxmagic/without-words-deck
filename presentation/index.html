Introduction

We've read all ten — start to finish.

Thanks for trusting us with the scripts. This is what we'd do with the show, how we'd keep it calm and on-spec across a ten-episode run, and how the budget would be built so you can shape it to the show you want to make.
What we found

A contained show where the effects should be felt, never seen.

  • The instinct here is restraint.Character-led and tightly held — the VFX should disappear into the story, not announce itself.
  • Most of the work is clear, and right in our lane.The texting that runs through every episode, the invisible craft around the performances, a clean season-wide finish.
  • Two creative decisions sit on top and shape everything.We've already mapped them — so when a director boards, you're choosing from a clear menu, not discovering surprises in post.
The work at a glance

Five moving parts — and one of them is the big lever.

  • Phone & screen graphics — the texting, calls, on-screen content, across all ten episodes the steady volume of the show
  • Invisible craft — the crucifixion harness, the stone-and-blood moment, screens and reflections seams the audience never sees
  • The performances — how the gospel scenes are finished the single biggest lever on the budget
  • Makeup continuity — the white-face holding across long performance days
  • Titles & on-screen text — series title, episode cards, supers

Our approach to your show

Where the work — and the money — actually lives.

The texting
A production decision, not just post
You raised the real fork yourself — on the phone screen vs the viewer's screen (your Mom's Night Out reference). The catch: the on-screen version needs clean shots of the phones grabbed on the day. We'd lock one approach before the camera rolls — a style choice, never a costly fix later.
The performances
The one big lever — your call, up front
Shot as a grounded filmed performance, or lifted with light, atmosphere and the supernatural. We present it as a clear choice now — not a number that quietly balloons once the look is set.
The invisible work
Seams nobody sees
The harness, the blood on white face-paint, the projected verses and screens — handled so they read as real and never pull a viewer out of the moment.
How we'd structure the options

Three packages — you choose how far the look goes.

Foundation recommended start
Screen inserts, the texting and device work, clean-up. Everything the scripts need as written, finished so it disappears.
Enhanced
Foundation, plus performance enhancement, atmospherics, and a handful of signature moments.
Premium
The fully heightened, cinematic treatment — the supernatural, divine-look version, end to end.
Each tier contains the one before it. Our steer: start at Foundation, and lift only the moments that earn it.
